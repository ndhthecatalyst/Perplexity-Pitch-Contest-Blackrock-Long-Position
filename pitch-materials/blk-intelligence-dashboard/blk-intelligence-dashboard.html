Competitive Moat

Four surfaces no single incumbent can replicate

01 · Index & ETF
iShares Platform Dominance
$5.5T in ETF AUM across US and international markets. $527B record net inflows in FY2025. 12% organic asset growth, 13% organic base fee growth. No ETF platform operates at this scale — the distribution moat compounds with every new ETF launch and market downturn.
$5.5T ETF AUM
02 · Alternatives
Private Markets Infrastructure
GIP (~$12.5B) created the 2nd-largest infrastructure manager globally. HPS adds $118B in private credit AUM, scaling BLK's credit franchise above $220B. Combined $423.6B alternatives AUM with $91B unfunded commitments. No traditional AM peer has this private markets footprint.
$423.6B Alts AUM
03 · Technology
Aladdin Operating System
Aladdin underpins risk management for $21.6T of institutional assets across 200+ clients. ACV grew 31% in FY2025 (16% organic + Preqin). Preqin transforms Aladdin from a risk OS into a private-markets data marketplace. Switching costs: 12–24 months of institutional disruption per client.
$21.6T Aladdin AUM
04 · Active
Active & Multi-Asset Scale
$2.5T in institutional active AUM. LifePath target-date franchise drives wealth channel growth. Multi-asset outsourcing mandates generated $46B in net inflows in FY2025. Active strategies carry higher base fee rates — a critical buffer against passive fee compression across the total platform.
$2.5T Active AUM

BLK at ~22x adj. P/E sits between the 9.7x traditional AM floor and the 29–39x alternatives ceiling. As alternatives + technology revenue grows from ~18% toward 30%+ of total, the market will credit BLK at a platform premium. This multiple expansion is independent of AUM growth — it is a structural re-rating event driven by revenue mix.

McKinsey "Great Convergence" Thesis
"Traditional and alternative asset management are structurally merging — and the firms that span both will capture the economics of the combined industry."
BlackRock is the only manager of scale operating across all four surfaces simultaneously: index, active, alternatives, and technology. GIP, HPS, and Preqin are not opportunistic acquisitions — they are the execution of this convergence thesis. No peer can replicate this combination from scratch; the lead-time alone is five or more years.
